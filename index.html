French Toast


Ingredients:

  • 2 Slices bread
  • 2 Eggs
  • Cinnamon sugar (optional)
  • Butter for frying

Instructions:

  1. Whisk eggs.
  2. Dip both sides of the bread in the eggs.
  3. Heat butter in a pan.
  4. Fry each side until golden brown.
  5. Top with cinnamon sugar.

Pancakes


Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 water
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 tbsp melted butter
  • Vanilla essence

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, suagr, baking powder and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. Pour wet into dry ingredients and gently stir until just combined.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Lightly grease with butter.
  5. Pour ~1/4 cup batter per pancake onto the pan. Cook until bubbles form on the surface.
  6. Flip and cook another 1–2 minutes until golden and cooked through.
  7. Serve warm with your favourite toppings.

Waffles


Ingredients:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp backing powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 egg
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your waffle machine.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, suagr, baking powder and salt.
  3. in another bowl, whisk the egg, milk, vanilla and melted butter
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  5. Lightly grease the waffle iron.
  6. Pour batter into the waffle iron, close, and cook until golden.
  7. Serve warm with your favourite toppings

Over-night Oats


Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup oats
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1tsp honey
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • Fruit for topping

Instructions:

  1. Add oats, milk, yogurt, and chia seeds to a jar or container.
  2. Stir in honey and vanilla.
  3. Mix well, close the lid, and refrigerate overnight.
  4. In the morning, stir and add extra milk if it’s too thick.
  5. Top with fruit.

Stir Fry


Ingredients:

  • 250 g chicken breast (sliced thin) — or tofu for a veg option
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell pepper, carrot, broccoli, snap peas)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (or any cooking oil)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated (or 1 tsp ginger paste)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce or hoisin (optional)
  • 1 tsp sugar or honey
  • 1 tbsp cornflour (cornstarch) mixed with 2 tbsp water (slurry)
  • Cooked rice or noodles, to serve
  • Chopped spring onion or sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Mix soy sauce, oyster/hoisin (if using), and sugar/honey in a small bowl — set aside.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add sliced chicken (or tofu) and stir-fry until almost cooked through — about 3–4 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the pan, then add garlic and ginger; stir for 20–30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry 3–4 minutes until tender-crisp (add harder veg like carrots first if needed).
  6. Return chicken to the pan and pour the sauce over. Toss to combine.
  7. Pour in the cornflour slurry, stir until sauce thickens and everything is coated (30–60 seconds).
  8. Turn off heat, taste and adjust seasoning (more soy for saltiness, a splash of water if too thick).
  9. Serve immediately over rice or noodles and garnish with spring onion or sesame seeds.

Tuna Salad


Ingredients:

  • 1 can tuna (in water or oil, drained)
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp chopped onion or spring onion (optional)
  • 1 tbsp chopped pickles or gherkins (optional)
  • 1 handful lettuce or spinach (for serving)
  • Bread, wraps, or crackers (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Drain the tuna well and add it to a bowl.
  2. Add the mayonnaise and lemon juice, then mix until combined.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Mix in onions or pickles if you want extra crunch and flavour.
  5. Serve the tuna salad on bread, wraps, crackers, or over a bed of lettuce.

Pizza


Ingredients:

  • 250 g strong bread flour (or all-purpose)
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 160 ml warm water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (plus extra for brushing)
  • 4–6 tbsp tomato passata or pizza sauce
  • 100–150 g shredded mozzarella (or cheese of choice)
  • Toppings: sliced tomato, ham, mushrooms, bell pepper, olives, basil — choose what you like

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl mix flour, yeast, sugar and salt.
  2. Stir in warm water and olive oil until a shaggy dough forms; knead 5–7 minutes until smooth (or use a stand mixer).
  3. Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover and let rise 45–60 minutes until roughly doubled (or 20–30 minutes in a warm spot).
  4. Preheat oven to 230°C (450°F). If you have a pizza stone, preheat that too.
  5. Punch down dough, shape into a circle on a floured surface, and stretch/roll to your desired thickness.
  6. Transfer to a floured baking tray or pizza peel. Brush edge with a little oil.
  7. Spread tomato sauce evenly, sprinkle cheese, then add toppings.
  8. Bake 10–12 minutes until crust is golden and cheese bubbly.
  9. Remove, let rest 2 minutes, add fresh basil if using, slice and serve.

Mince Pasta


Ingredients:

  • 250–300 g mince (beef, lamb or chicken)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1–2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (400 g) chopped tomatoes or passata
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste (optional)
  • 1 tsp mixed herbs or dried oregano
  • 1 tsp paprika (optional)
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • 1–2 tbsp oil
  • 250 g dried pasta (penne, spaghetti, fusilli)
  • Grated cheese to serve (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta according to packet directions; drain and set aside (reserve a splash of pasta water).
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat, sauté chopped onion until soft.
  3. Add garlic for 30 seconds, then add mince and brown, breaking it up with your spoon.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, mixed herbs and paprika.
  5. Stir in tomato paste (if using), then add chopped tomatoes/passata; simmer 8–10 minutes.
  6. If sauce becomes too thick, add a little pasta water to loosen it.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning, then toss pasta into the sauce until well coated.
  8. Serve with grated cheese and extra herbs if desired.

Carrot Cake


Ingredients:

  • 2 cups (250 g) plain/all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (200 g) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) brown sugar (or use all white sugar)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) vegetable oil (or melted butter)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups finely grated carrots (about 3 medium carrots)
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or raisins (optional)
  • For cream cheese frosting: 200 g cream cheese, 50 g butter softened, 1 cup (120 g) powdered sugar, 1 tsp vanilla

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease and line an 20–22 cm (8–9 in) round or 20x20 cm square pan.
  2. In a bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk sugars, eggs, oil and vanilla until smooth.
  4. Fold dry ingredients into wet until just combined (don’t overmix).
  5. Stir in grated carrots and nuts/raisins if using.
  6. Pour batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top.
  7. Bake 30–40 minutes until a skewer comes out clean (time varies by pan); cool completely.
  8. To make frosting: beat cream cheese and butter until smooth, add powdered sugar and vanilla, beat until creamy. Spread over cooled cake.

Cinnamon Rolls


Ingredients:

  • 3/4 cup (180 ml) warm milk
  • 2 1/4 tsp (1 packet) instant yeast
  • 1/4 cup (50 g) sugar
  • 1/4 cup (60 g) melted butter (plus extra)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 cups (250 g) all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • Filling: 1/3 cup (70 g) brown sugar, 2 tsp ground cinnamon, 2 tbsp softened butter
  • Optional glaze: 1 cup (120 g) powdered sugar + 1–2 tbsp milk + 1/2 tsp vanilla

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine warm milk, yeast and a pinch of sugar. Let sit 5 minutes until frothy.
  2. Stir in remaining sugar, melted butter and salt. Add flour and mix until a soft dough forms.
  3. Turn onto a floured surface and knead 4–6 minutes until smooth. Place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover and let rise 45–60 minutes until doubled.
  4. Roll dough into a rectangle about 30x25 cm (12x10 in). Spread softened butter over the surface.
  5. Mix brown sugar and cinnamon and sprinkle evenly over buttered dough.
  6. Roll tightly from the long edge into a log. Cut into 8 equal pieces and place cut-side-up in a greased baking dish (leave space between rolls).
  7. Cover and let rise 20–30 minutes. Preheat oven to 190°C (375°F).
  8. Bake 18–22 minutes until golden and cooked through. Cool slightly.
  9. Mix glaze ingredients and drizzle over warm rolls, or serve plain.
